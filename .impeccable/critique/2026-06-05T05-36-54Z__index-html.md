## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** The page doesn't immediately read AI-generated — the orange accent (#EE4408), Rifton display font, and committed dark palette give it a genuine POV. But two slop tells persist:

1. **Inter used for all substance headings.** Rifton appears in the nav logo, the product-flow display heading ("Voici CARRY IT"), and the manifest, but every content heading in the product sections — "Avant d'avancer", "Une ambition ne se pilote pas à l'aveugle", "Être occupé ne veut pas dire avancer", "Relier l'exécution à la vision", "La lucidité à court terme, la vision à long terme" — uses Inter 800 uppercase. Inter is the most over-deployed sans-serif on AI-generated pages. These headings carry the brand's "direct, lucid, demanding" voice. Inter mutes that.

2. **Tracked uppercase eyebrows on every section.** The market-gap has "LE PROBLÈME" in orange small caps. Every product step has "1. Objectif SMART", "2. Jalons", "3. KPI", "4. Tâches", "Dashboard" as tag-uppers with 0.72rem uppercase tracking. The numbered ones are partially justified (actual sequence), but the visual pattern repeats on 5+ sections and reads as AI scaffolding rather than a named brand system.

**Deterministic scan:** 3 findings, all true positives.
- `index.html:624` — `.section-text h2` explicitly sets `font-family: 'Inter'`
- `index.html:795` — `#smart` override repeats the Inter declaration
- `index.html:1585` — `.product-proof-title` hard-codes `font-family: 'Inter'`

All three are in high-visibility headings. No false positives.

**Browser visualization:** Live server started on port 8400; overlay injection skipped (no browser automation in this session).

## Overall Impression

The dark palette and Rifton bring genuine voice. The product story — ambition → structure → measurement — is clear and well-ordered. But two structural problems pull it back: the page explains the product twice (systemPreviewSection + productFlow cover the same Vision→Jalons→Execution chain at different zoom levels), and Inter-as-headings throughout flattens the typographic personality that Rifton establishes in the nav and hero. The single biggest opportunity: replace Inter in content headings with something that carries the brand's character.

## What's Working

1. **Rifton + #EE4408 commit to an identity.** Not a generic SaaS palette. The nav logo, hero title, and manifest heading use Rifton italic and the orange accent cleanly. The direction is right.

2. **Product flow narrative structure is sound.** SMART → Jalons → KPI → Tâches → Dashboard follows the actual product logic. Each panel shows a mockup adjacent to the copy. The dashboard panel with tabs is the most interactive and most memorable part of the page.

3. **Motion is disciplined.** IntersectionObserver reveals, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected throughout, count-up animation on KPI section, overdrive stagger on the final section. No gratuitous scroll animations.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Inter used for all content headings — the brand's typographic voice goes flat mid-page**
- **Why it matters:** Rifton is the brand's personality, but every substantive section heading falls back to Inter 800 uppercase. The product flow section explicitly overrides `font-family` to Inter (lines 624, 795). The "direct, lucid, demanding" brand voice is delivered in the same font on a million SaaS pages.
- **Fix:** Remove the explicit `font-family: 'Inter'` overrides on `.product-flow-section .section-text h2` (line 624), `#smart` (line 795), and `.product-proof-title` (line 1585). Either inherit Rifton or choose a dedicated body-heading face that isn't on the reflex-reject list.
- **Suggested command:** /impeccable typeset

**[P1] Two consecutive product explanation sections create structural redundancy**
- **Why it matters:** `#systemPreviewSection` (the Vision→Jalons→Execution chain mockup) and `#productFlow` (SMART, Jalons, KPI, Tâches, Dashboard panels) both show the product. The first preview covers the same ground the full flow expands on. Doubles scroll depth before the manifest CTA. Penalizes the high-achieving ICP who will feel walked through the same thing twice.
- **Fix:** Remove `#systemPreviewSection` or convert it into a true narrative bridge (why the product exists) rather than a product preview. The "Ce que je propose" bridge div at the end of market-gap already signals transition — lean into that instead.
- **Suggested command:** /impeccable distill

**[P2] Section rhythm is visually monotonous**
- **Why it matters:** `#080809`, `#111113`, `#0c0c0e`, `#09090a` alternate through the page but the delta is barely perceivable at scroll speed. The user experiences a homogeneous dark tunnel. The orange accent does its best, but one accent color on 6+ sections creates repetition rather than rhythm. No section feels decisively different.
- **Fix:** Give one section — the manifest CTA or the pain section — a meaningfully different background treatment. The manifest already has a glow; push it further (stronger orange wash, a near-full #EE4408 band). Or give the pain section a contrasting background to mark it as the empathy register.
- **Suggested command:** /impeccable colorize

**[P2] Pain section has register inconsistency and typos**
- **Why it matters:** The casual lowercase voice ("oklm", "aled", "vraiiiiiiment") is intentional for empathy. But "Parceque" appears twice (should be "Parce que") as a typo. The switch from lowercase casual to professional uppercase-accented French in the next section reads as inconsistency rather than intentional design without a visual signal separating the two registers.
- **Fix:** Fix the "Parceque" typos. Add a visual signal that marks this section as the empathy register (different background, no eyebrow, no number) so the register shift reads as deliberate.
- **Suggested command:** /impeccable clarify

**[P3] `.hero-subtitle` CSS exists but element is absent from HTML**
- **Why it matters:** The hero layout CSS references `.hero-subtitle` in margin calculations (`.hero-title + .hero-subtitle { margin-top: 104px }`). The element is not in the HTML. The layout spacer logic no longer applies. Dead CSS creates confusion about whether the element was removed intentionally.
- **Fix:** Either add the subtitle element back, or remove all CSS selectors referencing `.hero-subtitle`.
- **Suggested command:** /impeccable polish

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer)**

Lands on the hero. "Structure ton ambition en objectifs clairs et mesurables" is abstract. "Structure" how? Jordan doesn't know what SMART, Jalons, or KPI mean yet. Clicks "Voir le système" and lands at the product flow which opens with "Avant d'avancer" and a SMART builder mockup — still no sentence saying what CarryIT IS before showing step 1 of 4. The system-preview bridge ("CARRY IT relie ton ambition long terme à ton exécution quotidienne") does this job, but it's easy to scroll past sandwiched between competitors and the full product flow.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**

Hero works on mobile (separate title variant, full-width buttons). But 5 full product-flow panels on mobile — each collapsing to single column — is a very long scroll before the manifest CTA. No intermediate CTA between the market-gap section and the manifest. Casey exits before the CTA.

**Red flag:** Missing mid-page CTA. A second CTA placement after the system preview or after panel 2-3 would catch interrupted visitors.

**Nils / ICP (Project-Specific)**

High-achiever who already knows exactly what he wants. The two consecutive product explanation sections (system preview + full flow) add redundancy he will notice. The system preview shows Vision→Jalons→Execution chain; the full flow covers the exact same chain in more detail. The patient first-timer may benefit from two rounds; the CarryIT ICP finds it repetitive.

## Minor Observations

- `aria-hidden="true"` missing from hero scroll indicator (decorative SVG + text)
- `navbar-title` class styled in CSS but the element is absent from HTML navbar — dead CSS
- Font-weight values `650`, `680`, `850` throughout assume Inter's variable weight axis is loaded. Verify the loaded font includes `wght` variation axis; otherwise these snap to 600/700/800
- "Made with grit" at the bottom of the manifest is `font-size: 11px; opacity: 0.2` — nearly invisible. If intentional (easter egg), fine; if meant to be read, fix contrast
- Demo tab widget in `#ajustement` starts on "Jalons" tab (`showTab(1)` hardcoded). Users arriving fresh see mid-flow, not "Vision" first
